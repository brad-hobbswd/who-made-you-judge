About the Book

Who Made You Judge? is an invitation to return to the teachings of Jesus Christ with humility, wisdom, and compassion.

Throughout Scripture, Jesus never compromised truth, yet He continually demonstrated mercy toward broken people. He confronted sin without condemning sinners and called His followers to examine their own hearts before judging others.

This book carefully explores what the Bible teaches about judgment, discernment, grace, forgiveness, accountability, restoration, and spiritual maturity. Rather than encouraging criticism, it challenges readers to become ambassadors of Christ who speak truth in love.

Whether you have walked with Christ for many years or are just beginning your faith journey, these pages are designed to encourage deeper reflection and a greater understanding of God's heart for His people.

Why This Book Matters

Our world has become increasingly divided by criticism, assumptions, and quick opinions. Social media, conversations, and even churches can become places where judgment speaks louder than grace. Yet Jesus consistently demonstrated another way.

This book was written to encourage believers to return to Scripture and rediscover the heart of Christ. Every chapter points readers back to biblical truth while emphasizing humility, mercy, accountability, and restoration.

The goal is not to lower God's standard, but to raise our understanding of His compassion. When truth and grace walk together, lives are transformed.

Meet Bradley Hobbs

Bradley Hobbs is a Christian teacher, writer, and ministry leader with a passion for helping others grow in biblical understanding and spiritual maturity.

His heart is to encourage believers to seek truth through Scripture while demonstrating the grace and compassion that Jesus modeled throughout His earthly ministry.

Drawing from years of teaching, ministry, and personal reflection, Bradley writes with a desire to strengthen the Church and point readers toward a deeper relationship with Jesus Christ.
