About the Book

Who Made You Judge? is a biblical invitation to rediscover the balance of truth and grace through the teachings of Jesus Christ.

In a culture where criticism often replaces compassion, this book explores what Scripture truly says about judgment, discernment, forgiveness, accountability, humility, and restoration. Each chapter encourages readers to examine their own hearts before speaking into the lives of others.

Rather than promoting condemnation, these pages point readers toward the example of Christ, who never compromised truth while continually extending mercy. His ministry demonstrated that grace and holiness are never enemies but work together to reveal the heart of God.

Whether you are beginning your walk with Christ or have served Him for many years, this book is written to encourage spiritual growth, biblical understanding, and a deeper love for Jesus.

Why This Book?

Why This Book Matters

We live in a time when opinions spread instantly, assumptions are made without understanding, and criticism often speaks louder than compassion. Sadly, this spirit can even find its way into the Church, where believers are called to represent the heart of Christ.

Jesus never lowered God's standard, yet He consistently demonstrated mercy toward those who were hurting, broken, and searching for hope. He confronted sin without humiliating people and invited them into a transformed life through grace and truth.

Who Made You Judge? was written to encourage readers to return to Scripture and rediscover the example of Jesus. Each chapter challenges us to replace self righteousness with humility, condemnation with compassion, and personal opinions with biblical wisdom.

This is more than a book about judgment. It is an invitation to become more like Christ in the way we think, speak, serve, forgive, and love others.

Meet the Author

Bradley Hobbs

Bradley Hobbs is a Christian teacher, author, and ministry leader whose passion is helping people discover the life changing truth found in God's Word. Through years of teaching, mentoring, and writing, he has encouraged believers to grow in spiritual maturity while reflecting the compassion and character of Jesus Christ.

His writing combines biblical teaching with practical application, challenging readers to pursue truth with humility and extend grace without compromising Scripture. His desire is to strengthen the Church by pointing people back to the heart of Christ.

Who Made You Judge? reflects that calling. Every chapter invites readers to look beyond opinions and traditions, returning instead to the example of Jesus as revealed through Scripture.
